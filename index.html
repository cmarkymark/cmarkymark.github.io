Tweeting Under the Influence

It has been hypothesized that alcohol-related twitter data can be utilized as a proxy measure for actual acohol consumption. This project was designed to test this hypothesis by collecting and analyzing alcohol-related twitter data from San Diego, CA.

What To Do?

With new technologies have come new possibilities for leveraging big data to understand human behavior.

If there is a way to utilize social media data to identify and predict alcohol misuse, we can empower the public health sector to better address today's alcohol misuse crisis.

Our goal is to determine if twitter data can help predict alcohol consumption behaviors.

The Results

Tweet Heatmap

This map of San Diego displays a heatmap of alcohol-related tweets collected, dynamically displaying tweets by the time they were posted. Let the animation play to watch as new tweets pop up, but be sure to do the following as well : First, pause the animation; Second, highlight the entire timeline on the bottom of the map; Third, zoom in on the concentrated hotspots on the map. If you zoom in on the Gaslamp Quarter or North Park or Pacific Beach or SDSU, you will see a concentration of tweets. These are locations where consuming alcohol is socially normative and expected. This indicates that alcohol-related twitter data corresponds to incidence of individuals actually drinking!

Tweets By Day And Hour

The top graph shows the number of tweets by the hour of the day. In this graph we see that the rate of alcohol-related tweeting is highest in the late afternoon and evenings. The bottom graphs shows the average number of tweets per day of the week. Here we see that there are more alcohol-related tweets on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday than on the Weekdays. Given that drinking is more prevalent on weekends than on weekdays and that typically people drink in the evenings, this suggests that alcohol-related tweets reflect when people are drinking!
Next

We ran a Latent Dirichlet Allocation analysis on the tweets we collected to determine if we could classify the tweets by topic

Click here to view a graphic of this analysis

There are two applications for which LDA is important. The first is that we can differentiate between different types of alcohol-related tweets, such as individual use and business advertisements. The second is that it can be utilized to further clean the data of non-alcohol-related tweets, noting how certain groups are not alcohol-related

Next

One of the groups in our LDA analysis had the word "drink" as its primary group. It appears based on other terms in that group that this group is businesses trying to sell alcohol, saying things such as "Drink our beer." The following map shows this group as martini glasses and the rest as red dots. Notice how concentrations of red dots and martini glasses occur. This indicates that people tweeting about alcohol are doing so around the physical locations where alcohol is being sold.
